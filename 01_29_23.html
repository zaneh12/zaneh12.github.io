Stochastic Calculus

This week was the beginning of reading Stochastic Calculus for Finance I. The focus was on Binomial No-Arbitrage Pricing Model. The basis here is the condition that asset prices need to be determined in order to ensure no-arbitrage. In the simple fixed parameter binomial model, this condition is met by ensuring that the return of an asset is constrained by d < 1+r < u, ie the risk free rate of return neither exceeds the upside of an investment over the same time period nor falls below the downside. Pricing options can be done by attempting to take the other side of the trade using a hedging strategy in the stock and money market, also can be done by hedging a short position in a derivative security.

Chapter 2 followed more probability, in that the discussion of Martingales were introduced. Simply in this case a martingale is an adaptive stochastic process that says the value of the martingale at timepoint n is equal to the expectation of the martingale at timepoint n+1.
